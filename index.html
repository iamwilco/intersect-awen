§ 05 · Caveats

How to read these numbers.

No single hub leads on every metric, and no hub should. Structural differences between regions produce different efficiency profiles — reading any cost figure in isolation is misleading without context.

01

Hub durations differ

North America launched 17 September 2024 (~9.4 months active). Japan, LATAM, Sri Lanka, and WADA ran 12–14 months. Fixed contract amounts spread over fewer months produce higher per-month figures regardless of effort or output.

02

Local cost structures differ

$1 of contract value buys meaningfully more hub-manager time and venue capacity in Sri Lanka, Argentina, Ghana, or Kenya than in the US, EU, or Japan. Comparing per-event costs across regions without adjusting for purchasing power is misleading — and no meaningful adjustment exists in the dataset.

03

Sub-hub structure differs

LATAM runs 5 sub-hubs; WADA covers 3 sub-regions; Japan hosts weekly X Spaces across 6 cities; NA has one country, one manager, and ran fewer but larger flagship events (GM2025 DRep Debate, Consensus Toronto). Event counts are not unit-comparable across these shapes.

04

Snapshot lag

Per-hub event counts come from a 12 March 2025 presentation. NA's count here (18) is the hub's own end-of-program KPI; the snapshot showed 12. Other hubs likely have higher end-of-program counts than the snapshot captured — which would lower their per-event figures further.

05

No single "winner"

Japan leads on cost-per-member and cost-per-event. WADA leads on event volume and cost-per-event. North America leads on cost-per-milestone and is second on cost-per-member. LATAM funded the most expensive sub-hub launch programme from one contract. Sri Lanka led on educational throughput. Each hub produced a different shape of return — that is the design, not a bug.

Membership

Membership growth

No membership targets were set in contracts. Hubs collectively produced 1,268 individual members, 1,111 associates, and 16 enterprise accounts — 70% of Intersect's total individual membership. Japan alone contributed 30% of all individual members.

Geography

Sub-hub expansion

Geographic expansion was not a contract requirement. Hubs organically established 8 sub-hubs — WADA across Kenya, DRC, Cameroon, Liberia, South Sudan; LATAM across Uruguay and Mexico; NA across regional community nodes.

Education

University programmes

Not a contract deliverable. Sri Lanka built university partnerships producing 700+ students trained, including UniHack 2025 (225 students, 61 teams, 18 universities) and Uni Kelaniya (108 certified). This created a Cardano developer pipeline from nothing.

Governance

Governance participation

Contracts required event hosting but not governance-specific outcomes. Hubs delivered budget workshops, DRep debates, constitutional translations, GovTool engagement (12+ proposals reviewed by NA alone), and CC election support — enabling on-chain participation across regions.

Localization

Translation & localization

Content delivered in 8 languages — English, Japanese, Spanish, Portuguese, Sinhala, Tamil, French, Swahili. Japan translated the full Constitution, governance materials, and Intersect videos. Sri Lanka produced trilingual content. WADA ran across three working languages.

Voluntary

Financial transparency (Japan)

Not required by any contract. Japan voluntarily published a full itemized cost breakdown ($19,049 actual spend) and returned 15,436 ADA in unused funds to the treasury. This level of disclosure is now the baseline standard proposed for the 2026 framework.

§ 07 · FAQ

Community concerns — answered with receipts.

These are the questions people actually raised during and after the 2025 cycle. Honest, specific answers — not PR. Every figure cited is sourced from the full post-mortem.

Financial accountability
"Hubs had no financial accountability. We have no idea where the money went." +

The hub program had two funding phases and both are now publicly documented.

  • Phase 1 (~6 months): Funded by CDH and Intersect operational budgets. Contract amounts — $25K to $42K per hub, $161,000 USD total — are now disclosed via archived contract records.
  • Phase 2 (Apr–Jun 2025 bridge): Funded through public treasury withdrawal. Every payment is on-chain public record.
  • AMM event contracts: Issued separately per region for the Annual Members Meeting, also published.
  • Milestone-gated: 48 Milestone Acceptance Forms, DocuSign-verified, required before any payment was released — in both phases.

What remains outstanding from most hubs is itemized spend breakdowns. That was a contract design gap, not wrongdoing. The 2026 framework requires itemized reporting from every hub, by default, from day one.

"Only Japan was transparent. The other five hubs are financial black boxes." +

Phase 1 contract totals are now publicly disclosed for every hub (see § 02 — Funding). What Japan provided that others did not was an itemized spend breakdown: events 54.9%, staff 25.7%, hub manager 18.3%, tools 1.1%. That level of granularity was not a contract requirement for any hub in 2025.

Japan's standard is the new floor. The 2026 framework makes itemized financial reporting mandatory across every hub, written into the contract from the start.

"Where's the accounting for LATAM's 23k ADA? Sri Lanka's 18k?" +

Phase 1 contract totals are public: LATAM $42,000 USD, Sri Lanka $36,000 USD. Phase 2 bridge payments (LATAM 23,438 ADA, Sri Lanka 18,182 ADA) are on-chain public record. What remains unavailable is internal itemized breakdowns — not because they were hidden, but because the original contracts did not require them.

Japan's actual-spend benchmark — $19,049 across events, staff, management, and tools — remains the most useful public reference for what hub operations cost. 2026 contracts require this level of reporting from every hub.

Program value & ROI
"We don't have enough data to know if hubs were worth the investment." +

We now have both funding and outcomes. Headline numbers:

  • $161,000 USD Phase 1 contracts produced 1,249 individual members across five USD-contracted hubs — $128.90 per member.
  • 164 events delivered at an average cost of $982 per event.
  • Japan: 533 members from 61 events at $19,049 actual spend — $35.74 per member, with 15,436 ADA returned to treasury.
  • North America: 239 individual + 6 enterprise members from $25K in 9 months — $104.60 per member.
  • 70% of Intersect's individual members are in hub regions. These are active, committed members, not passive attendees.

Average monthly cost per hub was ~$2,640 — less than a single part-time contractor in most markets. These are community volunteer operations with modest budgets, not enterprise-scale programmes.

"Why did LATAM get $42K and North America only $25K? Who decided the amounts?" +

Phase 1 contracts were negotiated individually based on region size, launch date, scope, and existing local capacity. LATAM and Japan launched as founding hubs in May 2024 under the CDH structure with broader scope — including sub-hub launches across five countries for LATAM. North America launched in September 2024 with a narrower initial remit and delivered at a lower cost per member as a result.

In the 2026 proposal, the $75K regional hub pool is allocated against published KPIs and milestones rather than per-region flat contracts — matching allocation to demonstrated performance and regional scope.

"Europe's numbers look weak. Was it a failure?" +

Europe (Clay Nation) delivered 3 milestones against a different contract structure (ADA-denominated, 75,758 ADA) and a later launch date (September 2024). The lower milestone count reflects scope, not delivery failure — it was a smaller, shorter engagement by design. A like-for-like comparison with the six-hub average isn't meaningful.

Europe is excluded from USD ROI calculations specifically because its contract was ADA-denominated; including it would force exchange-rate assumptions into the dataset.

Governance & process
"The hub managers wrote their own post-mortem. Of course it looks good." +

The report is sourced from Milestone Acceptance Forms (DocuSign-verified), Intersect Development Updates #13–#70, 185+ public X posts, 30+ Luma event pages, 15+ video recordings, and archived Intersect contract pages — not self-report. Every funding figure has an on-chain or published-contract source. Every KPI claim links to a public artefact.

Cross-check any claim by following the citations in the full document.

"Why should we trust that 2026 will be different?" +

Three structural differences, not three promises:

  • Itemized financial reporting is a contract requirement, not an optional extra. Japan's 2025 standard becomes the 2026 floor for every hub.
  • Every funding request must state which KPI it supports, which milestone it contributes toward, and the expected measurable outcome — no open-ended allocations.
  • All funding flows through the Cardano treasury via the 2026 budget process, voted by DReps on-chain. That is independent, publicly verifiable oversight — not committee self-assessment.
