§ 02 · About the committee

Membership & Community Committee.

The MCC is an Intersect-facing committee focused on reviewing and improving the membership offering, and on growing and supporting participation across the Intersect community. We create the pathways — people and organizations join them.

01
I

Inclusivity

Champion diversity and keep governance tooling open to everyone, not only early insiders.

02
E

Empowerment

Support members to lead impactful initiatives in their regions and domains.

03
T

Transparency

Open dialogue and accessible decision-making, with every contract and KPI published.

04
C

Collaboration

Foster synergy across individuals, teams, and regions — not competition for finite attention.

05
S

Sustainability

Long-term engagement through responsible planning, not a sugar rush of one-off campaigns.

§ 03 · 2026 budget

Seven work packages. $300,000 USD.

Each work package maps to a concrete outcome: KPIs, milestones, and measurable impact. Funding is adaptable based on emerging needs and performance — every request must state which KPI it supports and what outcome it produces.

WP 01 $25,000

Membership Strategy & Growth

Strategy development & research ($10K), KPI dashboard & analytics tooling ($10K), membership framework & documentation ($5K).

WP 02 $30,000

Member Experience & Engagement

UX improvements for Virtual Hub & onboarding ($10K), engagement campaigns & tooling ($10K), community programs & incentives ($10K).

WP 03 $26,000

Member Education & Governance Enablement

Content creation — guides & docs ($8K), workshops & sessions ($10K), tooling & delivery platforms ($8K).

WP 04 $75,000

Global Community Hubs, Regional Enablement & Localization

Hub activity funding pool ($40K), regional coordination & oversight ($10K), performance tracking ($10K), translation services ($8K), contributor incentives ($5K), regional comms ($2K).

WP 05 $60,000

Community Initiative Enablement

Hackathon execution ($35K), marketing & outreach ($10K), mentorship & incubation ($10K), media & amplification ($5K). Includes Cardano India Hackathon as flagship model.

WP 06 $50,000

Membership Benefits & Partnerships

Partnership development ($10K), program management ($5K), benefit integration ($5K), Enterprise Member Directory — The Grid ($30K).

WP 07 $60,000

The Grid Events & Ecosystem Participation

Travel support fund ($20K), Intersect-led events ($15K), local hub events ($15K), branding, swag & NFTs ($10K).

Accountability clause

Every funding request under WP 01–07 must clearly state: which KPI it supports, which milestone it contributes toward, and the expected measurable outcome. No open-ended spending, no retrospective justification.

§ 06 · Participants

Who does what.

The MCC is one link in the Cardano governance chain. We don't act alone, and we don't make the final funding call — DReps do.

The committee

MCC

Designs programs, issues contracts, tracks performance, publishes data. Reports into Intersect and coordinates with the Steering Committee.

  • Program design & budget
  • Contracting & MAFs
  • KPI reporting
The regions

Hub operators

Deliver events, onboard members, localize content, run regional governance programming against agreed milestones and budgets.

  • Events & localization
  • Milestone delivery
  • Itemized financial reporting
The members

Community

Attend, contribute, translate, host sub-hub events, and vote inside Intersect governance. The program exists to serve them.

  • Participation & feedback
  • Sub-hub expansion
  • Working-group contribution
The sovereign

DReps

Vote on the 2026 Cardano budget. MCC funding only flows after DRep approval and the resulting on-chain treasury-withdrawal action.

  • Budget approval
  • On-chain voting
  • Accountability check
§ 07 · FAQ

Answering the community — directly.

The 2026 framework is designed as a direct response to legitimate community questions about 2025. These are honest, specific answers — not PR.

Financial accountability
"Hubs had no financial accountability. We have no idea where the money went." +

The hub program had two funding phases and both are now publicly documented.

  • Phase 1 (~6 months): Funded by CDH and Intersect operational budgets. Contract amounts are disclosed via Intersect's archived contract records — $25K to $42K per hub, totalling $161,000 USD.
  • Phase 2 (Apr–Jun 2025 bridge): Funded through public treasury withdrawal. Every payment is on-chain public record.
  • AMM event contracts: Separate contracts issued for Annual Members Meeting delivery per region, also published.
  • Milestone-gated: 48 Milestone Acceptance Forms, DocuSign-verified, required before any payment was released — in both phases.

What remains outstanding from most hubs is itemized spend breakdowns. That was a contract design gap, not wrongdoing. The 2026 framework requires itemized reporting from every hub, by default, from day one.

"Only Japan was transparent. The other five hubs are financial black boxes." +

Phase 1 contract totals are now publicly disclosed for every hub. What Japan provided that others did not was an itemized spend breakdown — events 55%, staff 26%, management 18%, tools 1%. That level of granularity was not a contract requirement for any hub in 2025.

Japan's standard is the new floor. The 2026 framework makes itemized financial reporting mandatory across every hub, written into the contract from the start.

"Where's the accounting for LATAM's 23k ADA? Sri Lanka's 18k?" +

Phase 1 contract totals: LATAM $42,000 USD, Sri Lanka $36,000 USD. Phase 2 bridge payments (LATAM 23,438 ADA, Sri Lanka 18,182 ADA) are on-chain public record. What remains unavailable is internal itemized breakdowns — not because they were hidden, but because the original contracts did not require them.

Japan's cost benchmark remains the most useful public reference for hub operations: ~$19,000 USD across events (55%), staff (26%), management (18%), and tools (1%). 2026 contracts require this level of reporting from every hub.

Program value & ROI
"We don't have enough data to know if hubs were worth the investment." +

We now have both the funding data and the outcomes data. Headline numbers:

  • $161,000 USD Phase 1 contracts produced 1,249 individual members across five USD-contracted hubs — $128.90 per member.
  • 164 events delivered at an average cost of $982 per event.
  • Japan: 533 members from 61 events at $19,049 actual spend — $35.74 per member, with 15,436 ADA returned to treasury.
  • North America: 239 individual + 6 enterprise members from $25,000 in 9 months — $104.60 per member.
  • 70% of Intersect's individual members are in hub regions. These are active, committed members, not passive attendees.

Average monthly cost per hub was ~$2,640 — less than a single part-time contractor in most markets.

"Why should we trust that 2026 will be any different from 2025?" +

Three structural differences, not three promises:

  • Itemized financial reporting is a contract requirement, not an optional extra. Japan's 2025 standard becomes the 2026 floor for every hub.
  • Every funding request must state which KPI it supports, which milestone it contributes toward, and the expected measurable outcome — no open-ended allocations.
  • All funding flows through the Cardano treasury via the 2026 budget process, voted by DReps on-chain. That is independent, publicly verifiable oversight — not committee self-assessment.
"Why did LATAM get $42K and North America only $25K? Who decided?" +

Phase 1 contracts were negotiated individually based on region size, launch date, scope, and existing local capacity. LATAM and Japan launched as founding hubs in May 2024 under the CDH structure with broader scope. North America launched in September 2024 with a narrower initial remit and delivered at a lower cost per member as a result.

For 2026, the $75K hub pool (WP 04) is allocated against published KPIs and milestones rather than per-region flat contracts — matching allocation to demonstrated performance and regional scope.

Governance & process
"Europe's numbers look weak compared to the other hubs. Was it a failure?" +

Europe (Clay Nation) delivered 3 milestones against a different contract structure (ADA-denominated, 75,758 ADA) and a later launch date (September 2024). The lower milestone count reflects scope, not delivery failure — it was a smaller, shorter engagement. A like-for-like comparison with the six-hub average isn't meaningful.

"Why should the same people run the program again?" +

Hub operators are selected per region based on demonstrated local capacity and delivery history, not legacy. Every 2026 contract is new, milestone-gated, and conditional on KPI performance. A hub that underdelivers in 2026 does not automatically renew — and the $75K WP 04 pool is explicitly distributed against measured performance, not seniority.

"The hub managers wrote their own post-mortem. Of course it looks good." +

The post-mortem is sourced from Milestone Acceptance Forms (DocuSign-verified), Intersect Development Updates #13–#70, public X/Luma/YouTube records, and archived Intersect contract pages — not committee self-report. Every funding figure has an on-chain or published-contract source. You can cross-check every claim by following the citations in the full document.

That's also why we're publishing this hub page — so the data is one click away, not buried behind five separate document systems.
